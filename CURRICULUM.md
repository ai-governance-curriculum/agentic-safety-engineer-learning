# Agentic Safety & Red-Team Engineer Curriculum

**Role level:** 40 (senior technical specialist — AI Governance family)
**Family:** AI Governance
**Status:** planned — modules and projects below are the planned scope authored from [`.aicg/curriculum-plan.json`](.aicg/curriculum-plan.json). Lessons and projects will be drafted by subsequent autonomous content cycles.

## Overview

This track teaches the *frontier-agent red-team craft* end-to-end for a senior specialist at level 40: reading frontier safety frameworks (Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy, OpenAI Preparedness Framework, Google DeepMind Frontier Safety Framework, EU GPAI Code of Practice, UK + US AI Safety Institute methodology) as an engineer would → threat modelling for autonomous / tool-using agents grounded in OWASP Agentic Threats + MITRE ATLAS → prompt-injection engineering across direct / indirect (retrieval, tool responses, long-term memory) / obfuscation vectors → jailbreak engineering at frontier depth (GCG, PAIR, TAP, Crescendo, many-shot, in-the-wild taxonomies) benchmarked against HarmBench, JailbreakBench, AIR-Bench 2024, MLCommons AILuminate, CyberSecEval → agent-specific attack surface (tool-abuse chains, memory / vector-store poisoning, long-horizon planning subversion, multi-agent adversarial coordination) benchmarked against AgentDojo + InjecAgent → dangerous-capability elicitation (CBRN with WMDP + Frontier Model Forum bio threat model, cyber-offense with Meta CyberSecEval 2/3, autonomous replication + R&D with METR public tasks + RE-Bench + SWE-bench Verified, persuasion, self-exfiltration) under proper security controls with external domain-expert graders → excessive-agency containment engineering (capability gates, sandboxed tool execution, principle-of-least-authority credentials, monitored-tool wrappers, human-in-the-loop escalation with bypass prevention, kill-switches with false-positive cost accounting) → frontier-scale guardrails and safety-monitor engineering (fine-tuned classifier guards, Constitutional Classifiers methodology, defence-in-depth composition, commercial-vendor build-vs-buy) → safety-case authoring in Goal Structuring Notation with Inability / Control / Trustworthiness / Deference argument shapes (Clymer et al., Goemans et al.) → AI control + adversarial-alignment evaluation (deception, alignment-faking, sandbagging, sleeper agents, in-context scheming, sabotage, model organisms of misalignment, Redwood-style control protocols) → automated + scaled red-teaming with UK AISI Inspect + PyRIT + garak, LLM-vs-LLM attacker loops, fine-tuned adversarial-attacker models, and StrongREJECT judge methodology → owning a frontier-safety program at organisation scope with RSP / Preparedness / FSF tier contributions, system-card + AISI + regulator-facing disclosure engineering, and serious-incident response.

It is a **senior technical-specialist track at level 40**, positioned in the **AI Governance family**, between the AI Evaluation Engineer / Security / AI Infra Security (peers / next-up at level 35, release-assurance methodology and platform-scale ML security) and the Senior AI Governance Architect (level 50, control-library architecture) → Head of AI Governance (level 60) → Chief AI Officer (level 70). The differentiator against the peer `ai-eval-engineer-learning` (AI Engineering family, level 30 — application-layer evaluation depth), against `model-evaluation-engineer-learning` (ML Engineering family, level 30 — statistical / benchmark methodology depth), and against `security-learning` / `ai-infra-security-learning` (peer / next-up, level 35 — platform-scale ML security) is *frontier-agent red-team ownership*: this curriculum builds the dangerous-capability elicitation methodology, the frontier-depth jailbreak + injection portfolio, the agent-attack surface engineering suite, the excessive-agency containment engineering, the AI-control + deception evaluation methodology, and the safety-case + system-card disclosure engineering.

The general engineering craft of AI risk — framework fluency at general depth, harm modelling, general adversarial-ML / fairness / privacy / guardrail engineering, MRM shape, risk registers, incident RCA, cross-functional program at team scope — is **prerequisite** and lives in [`ai-risk-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/ai-risk-engineer-learning) (level 25). This curriculum does not re-teach it.

Total planned commitment: **184 hours across 12 modules** + **130 hours across 3 projects** = **~314 hours**.

## Ownership rule

Following the project-wide ownership rule, this curriculum:

- **Owns** the frontier-agent red-team and dangerous-capability evaluation craft end-to-end — engineering-grade fluency with frontier safety frameworks, agent-specific threat modelling, prompt-injection engineering at frontier depth, jailbreak engineering (GCG / PAIR / TAP / Crescendo / many-shot / in-the-wild taxonomies), agent-specific attack surface engineering (tool-abuse chains, memory poisoning, long-horizon planning subversion, multi-agent adversarial coordination), dangerous-capability elicitation (CBRN, cyber-offense, autonomy, persuasion), excessive-agency containment engineering, frontier-scale guardrails and safety-monitor engineering, safety-case authoring in GSN, AI-control + adversarial-alignment evaluation, automated + scaled red-teaming, and the frontier-safety program + serious-incident-response + disclosure engineering slice.
- **Defers down** to [`ai-risk-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/ai-risk-engineer-learning) (level 25) as the **prerequisite track** — the general engineering craft of AI risk (framework fluency at general depth, harm modelling, general adversarial-ML / fairness / privacy / guardrail engineering, MRM shape, risk registers, incident RCA, cross-functional program at team scope) is **not re-taught** here; to [`ai-governance-analyst-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/ai-governance-analyst-learning) (level 15) for the operational analyst legwork below that; to [`ml-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ml-engineering-curriculum/ml-engineer-learning) (level 20) for classical ML / PyTorch / sklearn / MLflow / packaging fundamentals; to [`ai-infra-junior-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/ai-infra-junior-engineer-learning) (level 10) for engineering-craft prerequisites.
- **Defers sideways** to [`ai-eval-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ai-engineering-curriculum/ai-eval-engineer-learning) (peer, level 30, AI Engineering family) for application-layer LLM/agent evaluation-engineering plumbing (traces, judges, RAG eval, eval-gated CI/CD, online eval, cost / latency / quality); to [`model-evaluation-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ml-engineering-curriculum/model-evaluation-engineer-learning) (peer, level 30, ML Engineering family) for statistical calibration methodology (best-of-N CI, elicitation-gap accounting, judge-vs-human calibration); to [`fine-tuning-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ml-engineering-curriculum/fine-tuning-engineer-learning) (peer, level 30) for the post-training / safety-tuning stack this role tests for regressions; to [`security-learning`](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/security-learning) and [`ai-infra-security-learning`](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/ai-infra-security-learning) (peer / next-up, level 35) for deep ML/AI security at platform scale (model-extraction defence at inference edge, judge supply-chain, eval-set exfiltration prevention, MLSecOps, model-signing + provenance); to [`ai-evaluation-engineer-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/ai-evaluation-engineer-learning) (peer / next-up, level 35, Governance family) for release-assurance / audit-trail / regulator-facing methodology depth.
- **Defers up** to [`senior-ai-governance-architect-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/senior-ai-governance-architect-learning) (level 50) for control-library architecture, policy taxonomy, and cross-jurisdiction reconciliation; to [`head-of-ai-governance-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/head-of-ai-governance-learning) (level 60) and [`chief-ai-officer-learning`](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/chief-ai-officer-learning) (level 70) for program leadership, board-level reporting, and regulator engagement.
- **Out of scope** to legal counsel (this role does not deliver legal opinion); to security operations / DFIR (SOC-side incident handling; this role feeds AI-specific signal to SecOps); and to the frontier-lab AI-safety-research-scientist ladder (novel-alignment research at PhD-publication depth — this role *consumes* that research but does not compete for the same postings).

See [`JOB_REQUIREMENTS.md`](JOB_REQUIREMENTS.md) for the requirements-to-coverage map and the cited public references the catalog is grounded in.

## Module summaries

### mod-101 — Frontier Safety Frameworks and the Safety-Engineer Position
Read Anthropic RSP, OpenAI Preparedness, and Google DeepMind FSF as an engineer — mapping each ASL / Preparedness tier / Critical Capability Level to concrete elicitation protocols, tripwires, rollback contracts, and evidence artifacts. Read the EU GPAI Code of Practice safety chapter and EU AI Act Articles 55 / 56 / 73 as the regulatory shape this role feeds evidence into. Read US EO 14110, the Bletchley Declaration + Seoul Summit outcomes, and UK + US AISI pre-deployment methodology. Diagram this role's exact scope on the level ladder — what `ai-risk-engineer` (level 25) already owns as prerequisite and what `ai-evaluation-engineer` (level 35), `senior-ai-governance-architect` (level 50), and `head-of-ai-governance` (level 60) inherit or extend.

### mod-102 — Threat Modelling for Autonomous and Tool-Using Agents
Author an agent-specific threat model — data-input, tool-invocation, memory / vector-store, environment-observation, human-in-the-loop, and cross-agent surfaces — with adversary personas from script-kiddie jailbreaker through insider through nation-state uplift-seeker, grounded in the AI Incident Database and OECD.AI incidents. Layer OWASP Agentic Threats, OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS agent techniques, and Google SAIF pillars. Distinguish operator- / model- / data- / integration- / multi-agent-emergent harms. Reason about RSP / Preparedness / FSF as enterprise agent-deployment-tier shape templates.

### mod-103 — Prompt-Injection Engineering
Engineer direct prompt-injection attacks (instruction-override, refusal-bypass, role-hijack, task-override, exfiltration-through-completion, output-format hijack), indirect prompt-injection attacks through retrieved context / tool responses / long-term memory / cross-plugin channels (grounded in Greshake et al. and InjecAgent), and obfuscation vectors (unicode confusables, homoglyph, ASCII art, base64 / rot13 / cipher, low-resource-language translation). Ship a working evaluation harness with harmful-payload discipline (evals hosted outside the source repo). Author a prompt-injection defence catalogue — spotlighting, StruQ, sandwich prompting, self-refuting classifiers, tool-response sanitisation — and stress-test each against adaptive attacks.

### mod-104 — Jailbreak Engineering at Frontier Depth
Engineer white-box gradient-based jailbreaks (GCG universal + transferable adversarial suffixes); black-box iterative jailbreaks (PAIR, TAP, Crescendo multi-turn refusal-erosion) with LLM-vs-LLM attacker loops under rate-limit + cost budgets; many-shot in-context jailbreaks under long-context models; role-play / persona-hijack; low-resource-language and cipher attacks. Benchmark against HarmBench, JailbreakBench, AIR-Bench 2024, MLCommons AILuminate, Meta CyberSecEval with StrongREJECT-shape judge methodology. Translate in-the-wild DAN-family jailbreak taxonomies (Shen et al.) into permanent regression fixtures.

### mod-105 — Agent-Specific Attack Surface Engineering
Engineer tool-abuse chains (unintended tool invocation via injection, argument smuggling, chained exfiltration through search + email + calendar tools, credential-theft through code-interpreter); memory / vector-store poisoning (user-history writes, cross-session persistence, RAG-index poisoning, adversarial embedding-space attacks); long-horizon planning subversion (goal replacement, sub-goal injection, plan-truncation, sandbox-escape through plan-branching); multi-agent adversarial coordination (cross-agent injection, adversarial-message relay, principal-confusion in A2A / MCP protocols). Ship coverage against AgentDojo + InjecAgent with attack-success-rate + coverage matrix under different defensive postures.

### mod-106 — Dangerous-Capability Evaluation Engineering
Design a dangerous-capability elicitation protocol — capability-elicitation gap accounting, best-of-N sampling, fine-tune-elicitation, tool-use-elicitation, scaffolding — with pre-registered thresholds against RSP / Preparedness / FSF tiers. Engineer CBRN-uplift (WMDP + Frontier Model Forum bio threat model, external CBRN-domain-expert graders, IAEA / WHO coordination); cyber-offense (Meta CyberSecEval 2 + CyberSecEval 3, CTF suites, MITRE-ATT&CK-tagged autonomy tasks); autonomous replication + R&D (METR public tasks, RE-Bench, SWE-bench Verified with elicitation-gap accounting); persuasion / manipulation; self-exfiltration. Author a dangerous-capability report shaped for RSP / Preparedness / FSF review.

### mod-107 — Excessive-Agency Containment Engineering
Engineer capability gates (allow-list tool policies, argument validators, side-effect scopes, principle-of-least-authority credentials, per-tool rate limits, action budgets, blast-radius caps) grounded in OWASP LLM06 + Agentic Threats; sandboxed tool execution (gVisor / Firecracker / OS sandboxes for code-interpreter, network-egress allow-lists, filesystem overlay, ephemeral compute); monitored-tool wrappers (argument diffing, outcome verification, tamper-evident audit-log emission); human-in-the-loop escalation (thresholds, timeout defaults, revocable-consent, bypass-prevention against social engineering + fatigue + approval-bombing); kill-switches with false-positive cost accounting + escalation-fan-in.

### mod-108 — Frontier-Scale Guardrails and Safety-Monitor Engineering
Design a defence-in-depth guardrail architecture — pre-input filters, input classifiers (Llama Guard, ShieldGemma, OpenAI Moderation), output classifiers (Constitutional Classifiers, custom fine-tuned safety classifiers), rule / flow guards (NeMo Guardrails, Guardrails AI), post-tool-response validators, safety-monitor sidecars. Ship a fine-tuned classifier guard with training-data authoring + adaptive-attack survival curve. Adopt Anthropic Constitutional Classifiers methodology. Evaluate commercial vendors (Lakera Guard, HiddenLayer AIDR, Protect AI Guardian, Robust Intelligence, Calypso AI, HydroX AI) with a build-vs-buy matrix. Measure guardrail effectiveness with FP/FN + cost + latency + adaptive-attack survival. Defer general guardrail depth to `ai-risk-engineer` (prerequisite, level 25).

### mod-109 — Safety Cases and Structured Argumentation
Read Clymer et al. and Goemans et al. as the working templates for frontier-AI safety cases. Adopt Goal Structuring Notation (GSN Community Standard v3) as the structured argumentation format. Distinguish the four safety-case argument shapes — Inability, Control, Trustworthiness, Deference. Author a safety case for one concrete high-risk deployment, modelled on RSP / Preparedness / FSF tier decisions and AISI pre-deployment reports, with evidence types (dangerous-capability eval, red-team, control-eval, incident-history, alignment-check) explicitly cited. Reason about safety-case pitfalls (evidence-provenance gaps, argument-completeness gaps, elicitation-gap ambiguity, moving-target thresholds, rebuttal-handling).

### mod-110 — AI Control, Deception, and Adversarial-Alignment Evaluation
Engineer deception evaluations (Apollo Research methodology — self-reasoning tests, hidden-scratchpad probes, in-context deception, honesty probes); alignment-faking (Anthropic + Redwood methodology); sandbagging + capability-elicitation gap; sleeper-agent / trojan (Anthropic Sleeper Agents, backdoor-persistence-through-safety-tuning, trigger discovery); in-context scheming (Meinke et al.); sabotage (Anthropic sabotage-evals). Adopt the AI-control paradigm (Redwood) — safety despite an intentionally adversarial model — designing red-team-blue-team games and untrusted-monitoring baselines. Author model-organisms of misalignment for controlled study and hand off to `fine-tuning-engineer` (peer, level 30).

### mod-111 — Automated and Scaled Red-Teaming
Orchestrate a scaled red-team program with UK AISI Inspect + Microsoft PyRIT + NVIDIA garak + Promptfoo red-team — coverage matrix across attack technique × behaviour category × model version × decoding config. Engineer LLM-vs-LLM attacker loops (PAIR / TAP / RL-based); fine-tune a dedicated adversarial-attacker model on your organisation's failure-mode corpus with harmful-payload discipline. Adopt StrongREJECT-shape judge methodology and calibrate LLM-judge vs. human-judge. Engineer coverage + population-scale replay bundles with decoding + model + prompt + tool-response hashes pinned. Adopt harmful-payload storage discipline (private HF org / S3 with per-role IAM, redaction in logs + tickets, legal-review gate for CBRN / cyber-offense payloads).

### mod-112 — Frontier-Safety Program, Serious-Incident Response, and Disclosure
Own a frontier-safety + red-team program at organisation scope — cadence (pre-training / mid-training / pre-deployment / post-deployment safety-eval), artifacts (safety cases + system cards + AISI reports + regulator-facing incident reports), decisions (RSP tier / Preparedness scorecard / FSF critical-capability-level threshold), sign-off routing (Responsible-Scaling Officer / Preparedness Committee / Safety Board). Contribute to RSP / Preparedness / FSF tier decisions with pre-registered thresholds + elicitation-methodology defence + rollback contract. Author Anthropic / OpenAI / DeepMind-shape system cards, EU AI Act Article 73 serious-incident reports, EU GPAI Code of Practice safety-and-security disclosure, and UK + US AISI pre-deployment report contributions. Run serious-incident response with regulator + AISI coordination. Position the certifications portfolio (IAPP AIGP, ISO/IEC 42001 LI/LA, ForHumanity, BABL AI).
