## Methodology

1. Sourced the canonical task domains for an Agentic Safety & Red-Team Engineer (level 40, AI Governance family) from the public references catalogued in `.aicg/job-requirements.json → authoritative_references`.
2. Mapped each task domain to (a) the role on our level ladder that should own it primarily and (b) the curriculum module that will cover it.
3. Applied the **ownership rule** — assign coverage to the lowest-level role that genuinely requires the skill; higher-level tracks link back rather than duplicate. In particular:
   - *Down* to `ai-risk-engineer` (level 25) for the general engineering craft of AI risk that this role treats as prerequisite — framework fluency at general depth, harm modelling, general adversarial-ML / fairness / privacy / guardrail engineering, risk registers, incident RCA, cross-functional team-scope program.
   - *Down* to `ai-governance-analyst` (level 15) for operational analyst legwork (intake, inventory, regulatory tracking).
   - *Down* to `ml-engineer` (level 20) for classical ML fundamentals; to `ai-infra-junior-engineer` (level 10) for engineering-craft prerequisites.
   - *Sideways* to `ai-eval-engineer` (peer, level 30, AI Engineering family) for application-layer evaluation-engineering plumbing.
   - *Sideways* to `model-evaluation-engineer` (peer, level 30, ML Engineering family) for statistical / calibration methodology depth.
   - *Sideways* to `fine-tuning-engineer` (peer, level 30) for the post-training / safety-tuning stack this role tests.
   - *Sideways* to `security-learning` / `ai-infra-security` (peer / next-up, level 35) for platform-scale ML security.
   - *Sideways* to `ai-evaluation-engineer` (peer / next-up, level 35, Governance family) for release-assurance / audit-trail methodology.
   - *Up* to `senior-ai-governance-architect` (level 50) for cross-jurisdiction policy architecture; to `head-of-ai-governance` (level 60) + `chief-ai-officer` (level 70) for program leadership.
   - *Out of scope* to legal counsel, to SOC / DFIR, and to the frontier-lab safety-research-scientist ladder (PhD + NeurIPS/ICML publication depth).
4. Flagged everything that has not yet been validated against in-window postings so the next cycle can demote any requirement whose evidence stays empty.
