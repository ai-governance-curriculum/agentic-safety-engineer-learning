# 09 — Certifications Portfolio

## Motivation

At level 40 in the AI Governance family, the hiring signal that clears the bar is documented **contribution history**: RSP / Preparedness / FSF tier contributions, published system-card evidence, AISI-methodology contributions, Frontier Model Forum publication history. Certifications are a *supporting* signal, never the primary one — but the current market is credentialising quickly and a small, well-chosen portfolio helps clear filters.

This chapter names the certifications that are load-bearing (or emerging as load-bearing) at senior AI-safety hiring, and gives you a rubric for deciding which ones to invest in.

### IAPP AIGP — Artificial Intelligence Governance Professional

- **Publisher.** [International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP)](https://iapp.org/certify/aigp/).
- **Positioning.** IAPP's AI governance certification, launched November 2023. Covers AI governance principles, risk frameworks (NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001 at survey depth), model governance, and lifecycle risk.
- **Hiring signal at level 40.** **Highest-cited certification** in senior AI-safety and AI-governance postings. If you invest in one certification, this is the one that most frequently appears as "preferred" or "highly desired" in postings for this role.
- **What it is not.** It is not a frontier-safety-engineering certification. It surveys governance frameworks; it does not teach the elicitation methodology, red-team engineering, or safety-case authoring craft this track covers.
- **When to earn it.** Early — the exam cost is low relative to the signal, and passing it removes friction on filter screens.

### ISO/IEC 42001 lead-implementer / lead-auditor

- **Standard.** [ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI management system](https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html).
- **Publishers of the credential.** [PECB](https://pecb.com/en/education-and-certification-for-individuals/iso-iec-42001), [BSI](https://www.bsigroup.com/en-GB/products-and-services/training-courses/), IRCA, and others offering their own lead-implementer / lead-auditor curricula for 42001. Certification schemes are still shaking out.
- **Positioning.** Anchors to the ISO/IEC 42001 AI management-system standard. A **lead-implementer** credential signals you can build and operate the management system inside a provider organisation; a **lead-auditor** credential signals you can audit it. 42001 lead-auditor is emerging as the go-to auditor credential.
- **Hiring signal at level 40.** Emerging but rising fast. Providers that pursue ISO/IEC 42001 certification (increasingly common) look for the LI credential on hiring for governance-adjacent engineering roles. LA credential is more relevant for third-party auditors / consultants and for internal audit teams than for engineers on the safety side.
- **What it is not.** It is not a frontier-safety-engineering credential. 42001 is a management-system standard; it does not cover elicitation methodology or dangerous-capability evaluation depth.
- **When to earn it.** Later than AIGP. Only invest if (a) your employer or a target employer is pursuing 42001 certification, or (b) you are moving toward the level-50 architect role where cross-jurisdiction control-library architecture is the job.

### ForHumanity Independent AI Auditor

- **Publisher.** [ForHumanity](https://forhumanity.center/independent-audit-of-ai-systems/).
- **Positioning.** Independent-auditor credentials for AI systems, aligned to ForHumanity's audit criteria. Anchored to specific jurisdictional audit frames (EU AI Act, NYC Local Law 144, etc.).
- **Hiring signal at level 40.** Occasional citation. More common in independent-audit and consultancy postings than in in-house safety-engineering postings.
- **What it is not.** Not a substitute for the AIGP or 42001 credentials on hiring filters.
- **When to earn it.** If you are moving toward an independent-audit or consulting career path adjacent to this role, this is on the shortlist.

### BABL AI (Algorithm Auditor certifications)

- **Publisher.** [BABL AI](https://babl.ai/) — training and certification for algorithm auditors.
- **Positioning.** Algorithm-auditing certificates, with a specific bias-and-audit lean. BABL AI has been active on NYC Local Law 144 (bias audits) and adjacent bias-audit frames.
- **Hiring signal at level 40.** Rare in frontier-safety hiring, more common in bias-audit hiring. Useful where the role touches bias-adjacent audit obligations.
- **When to earn it.** Only if the role you are targeting has a bias-audit component. Otherwise, allocate the study time elsewhere.

## A rubric for deciding what to earn

At level 40, the hierarchy of *hiring* signal (as opposed to *learning* signal) is roughly:

1. **Documented contribution history** — published system-card contributions, RSP / Preparedness / FSF tier contribution history, AISI-methodology contribution, FMF working-paper co-authorship, published red-team writeups, published open-source safety tooling (Inspect solvers, PyRIT operators, garak probes). This is by far the strongest signal.
2. **Frontier-lab or top-flight AI-safety org employment history.** Anthropic Alignment, OpenAI Preparedness / Safety Systems, Google DeepMind Responsibility & Safety Engineering, Apollo, Redwood, METR, and equivalents.
3. **Peer-reviewed publications and preprints** in adversarial ML, LLM safety, agent safety, AI control.
4. **IAPP AIGP** — most-cited certification; passes filter screens.
5. **ISO/IEC 42001 lead-implementer / lead-auditor** — rising, particularly for orgs pursuing 42001 certification.
6. **Formal degree** — not required, but a graduate degree in a relevant field (ML, CS, security, cognitive science, HCI, philosophy of AI) helps.
7. **ForHumanity / BABL AI** — situational, useful if the job description asks for them.

**Recommended portfolio for level 40:** IAPP AIGP is the default. Add ISO/IEC 42001 lead-implementer if your organisation is pursuing 42001 or you are moving toward level 50. Consider ForHumanity or BABL AI only where a specific role asks for them.

## Positioning against neighbouring roles

- **`ai-risk-engineer` (level 25) prerequisite.** IAPP AIGP is the standard credential at level 25 as well; if you earned it at level 25 you do not need to re-earn it. The 42001 LI credential is more relevant at level 40 than at level 25.
- **`ai-evaluation-engineer` (level 35, Governance).** Overlaps heavily; AIGP is the shared default.
- **`security` / `ai-infra-security` (level 35).** Different portfolio — CISSP, GIAC, OSCP dominate; AI-specific credentials are secondary. If you are cross-training into that lane, invest in the security-side credentials rather than layering more AI ones.
- **`senior-ai-governance-architect` (level 50).** The 42001 lead-implementer / lead-auditor credential becomes more central. AIGP remains baseline.
- **`head-of-ai-governance` (level 60).** Certifications become less load-bearing than executive-track credentials (industry association leadership, published thought-leadership).

## A time budget

If you are at level 40 and choosing where to invest study time in a 12-month window:

- **AIGP:** 60–100 hours of study, one exam. High-leverage.
- **42001 lead-implementer:** 30–40 hours of course + exam. Medium-leverage.
- **A published FMF working-paper contribution or open-source Inspect eval:** 40–100 hours. **Highest-leverage of any of these** for the actual job. Prioritise it above the certifications if you have to choose.
- **A published red-team writeup or safety-case in a public system card contribution:** 40–100 hours. Same tier as the FMF contribution.

## Common misreadings to avoid

- **Substituting certifications for contribution history.** At level 40, contribution history dominates. A stacked cert wall with no contribution history reads as a filter-optimised candidate, not a hired candidate.
- **Substituting AIGP for framework fluency.** The IAPP AIGP survey coverage of NIST / ISO / EU AI Act is not the same as engineering-depth reading of the primary sources this module teaches. The credential complements the skill; it does not replace it.
- **Earning 42001 LI before you need it.** If your organisation is not pursuing 42001 certification and you are not moving toward level 50, the study time is better allocated to the FMF / open-source-contribution lane.
- **Over-investing in bias-audit credentials for a role that does not need them.** BABL AI is excellent in its domain; that domain overlaps only partially with frontier-safety engineering.

## Summary

- IAPP AIGP is the most-cited AI-governance certification and the default portfolio item at level 40.
- ISO/IEC 42001 lead-implementer / lead-auditor is emerging as the second-most-important credential, particularly for providers pursuing 42001 certification.
- ForHumanity Independent AI Auditor and BABL AI are situational, useful where a specific role asks for them.
- Contribution history — published system-card work, RSP / Preparedness / FSF tier contributions, AISI-methodology contributions, FMF working papers, open-source safety tooling — dominates any certification stack at level 40.
- Time allocation: earn AIGP early, layer 42001 LI as needed, prioritise contribution history above further certifications.
